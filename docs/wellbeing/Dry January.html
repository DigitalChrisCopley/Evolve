Are you one of the 4.2 million people who have already pledged to take part in Dry January? 

The campaign set up by the British charity, Alcohol Change, encourages us to give up alcohol for January. Now in its seventh year, the campaign is going from strength to strength. The initiative is not about giving up alcohol for good, but for the person who drinks socially to reflect on their drinking patterns and give their body a welcomed break from alcohol after the festive period. 

Not only is abstaining for a month good for our health and wellbeing, 70%of last years participants reported better sleep, 66% had more energy and 65% had generally improved better health. 

In addition it is a great way to save money with nearly 86% of last years participants saying they were better off financially at the end of the month. 

If you want to sign up to the Pledge or simply find out more information, check out Dry January on the Alcohol Change UK.
