Working from Home

 

When working from home the preferred and most reliable method is for your Council laptop to be connected directly to your router via a network cable (hardwired).  If this is not logistically possible, IT Services recommend the use of an Ethernet over Power adaptor.

 

The adaptor provides simplicity and reliability that wireless technology still can't match. It's not designed to replace a Wi-Fi network, it's supposed to complement one.  You will need a plug socket near your router and close to where you are working. Further information can be found in the Ethernet over Power adaptor guidance. 
 
 
When logging on to the Council network from home for the first time you will need to connect your laptop directly to your home Wi-Fi internet provider (IP) address. Once you have hardwired your laptop or connected to your home Wi-Fi, you will need to log in. How you connect to your home Wi-Fi and log in depends upon which device you have. Information on how to establish which device you have can be found in the identify my device guidance.
 
 
Once you have identified your device you will need to connect to your Wi-Fi and log in. If you have a Workspace laptop instructions can be found in the connecting to your Wi-Fi- and logging in from home workspace guidance. If your laptop is a non workspace device then you can find guidance in the logging in from home for a Windows 7 or for a Windows 10 laptop. 
 
 
If you are working remotely and have no Wi-Fi connection, you can create a Wi-Fi hotspot on your Council smartphone and tether your Workspace laptop directly to your phone. Information on how to create a Wi-Fi hotspot can be found in the Smartphone Wi-Fi hotspot guidance (this only works for Workspace laptops).
